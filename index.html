Hello!

My name is Amy Surrett. I grew up in Pennsylvania, although I spent most of my highschool years living in Lima, Peru. I'm now living in South Carolina and loving this area, though I thoroughly enjoy traveling.

I've been intrigued by computers my whole life but became interested in programming when I participated in the Hour of Code program my junior year of highschool. One hour soon turned into many hours and after graduating highschool and working for a year to pay for college, I decided to major in computer science. While attending Bob Jones University, I had the opportunity to work as an audio-visual technician and a computer lab monitor, and to participate in a few programming competitions.

Due to a couple of unforseeable events, I had to take another academic gap year in 2019-2020. This time period really helped me solidify my passion for programming, as I used the resources avaliable to me to continue my education on my own. I earned several certifications through freeCodeCamp and learned how to teach myself.

This year I have restarted my journey to a degree through WGU's software development program. Because of my previous college credits and WGU's unique educational model, I hope to finish my bachelors degree by May of 2022.
